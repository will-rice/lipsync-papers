## Talking Head Generation

The rapid evolution of multi-modal priors has catalyzed significant breakthroughs across diverse generative frontiers, . Early talking head generation methods convert speech into facial motion with lip-sync discrimination, head-pose prediction, and compact pose/emotion codes. Wav2Lip adds a lip-sync discriminator . Audio2Head predicts 6D head pose and dense motion from audio . PC-AVS learns a compact pose code . EAMM injects emotion offsets in the same latent space . Diffusion models  have become the dominant paradigm for audio-conditioned portrait videos, scaling to long duration, high resolution, and richer controls. DiffTalk, Diffused Heads, EMO, and Loopy generate from audio with latent or autoregressive diffusion . <span style="color: rebuttal">SadTalker generates 3D motion coefficients and implicitly modulates a 3D-aware face renderer. Sonic focuses on global audio perception, disentangling it into intra-clip and inter-clip audio knowledge.</span> DAWN and IF-MDM enable non-autoregressive sampling or compressed motion latents . Hallo, Hallo2, and Long-Term TalkingFace factorize lip, expression, and pose or introduce motion priors for long-form and 4K synthesis . AniPortrait, HunyuanPortrait, MODA, and PortraitTalk adopt two-stage or controllable pipelines . <span style="color: rebuttal">EDTalk introduced an efficient disentanglement framework that separately models mouth shape, head pose, and emotional expression through lightweight, orthogonal latent spaces.</span> EchoMimic and V-Express guide diffusion with sparse facial landmarks . LatentSync and EchoMimicV2 add alignment and conditioning modules . EMOPortraits reshapes the latent space to capture intense and asymmetric expressions . <span style="color: rebuttal">Recent works have explored leveraging high-level semantic cues from text or multimodal inputs. Wan-S2V builds upon a base model to achieve enhanced expressiveness for film-level cinematic contexts. MultiTalk addresses multi-person conversational video generation using a Label Rotary Position Embedding (L-RoPE) to solve audio-person binding. OmniHuman-1.5 utilizes Multimodal Large Language Models to synthesize structured textual guidance, enabling semantically coherent and expressive character animations beyond simple rhythmic synchronization.</span> Emotion adapters such as ETAU, EAT and SAAS attach a compact affect code to a frozen generator , while MEMO couples a memory-guided module with emotion-aware diffusion, sustaining identity and lip-sync across clips. <span style="color: rebuttal"> DICE-Talk introduces a disentangled emotion embedder and correlation-enhanced emotion conditioning for emotional portraits. Takin-ADA uses a two-stage approach with specialized loss and advanced audio processing to enhance subtle expression transfer and lip-sync precision.</span> In contrast to prior methods that rely on emotion labels or latent codes, our method AUHead, guides a diffusion model through temporally aligned AU features. This enables structured and interpretable control over fine-grained facial expression synthesis.

## LLMs for Audio Information Perception

Instruction-tuned Audio-Language Models (ALMs) endow large language models with a “sense of hearing”, enabling open-ended spoken interaction across speech, music, and ambient sounds . Qwen-Audio scales multi-task pre-training to over 30 tasks and introduces hierarchical tags to reduce cross-task label interference, establishing a strong open-source baseline . WavLLM adopts dual encoders plus curriculum learning to improve robustness on universal speech benchmarks , while SpeechVerse freezes foundation models and instruction-tunes a lightweight fusion layer, outperforming task-specific baselines on eleven benchmarks . While existing ALMs focus on general audio understanding, our method adapts them for a novel purpose: exploring the capacity of ALMs to understand and represent emotional cues through temporally aligned AU sequences.
