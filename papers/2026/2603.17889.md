# Identity as Presence: Towards Appearance and Voice Personalized Joint Audio-Video Generation

Yingjie Chen    Shilun Lin    Cai Xing    Binxin Yang    Long Zhou    Qixin Yan    Wenjing Wang    Dingming Liu    Hao Liu    Chen Li    Jing LYU

###### Abstract

Recent advances have demonstrated compelling capabilities in synthesizing real individuals into generated videos, reflecting the growing demand for identity-aware content creation. Nevertheless, an openly accessible framework enabling fine-grained control over facial appearance and voice timbre across multiple identities remains unavailable. In this work, we present a unified and scalable framework for identity-aware joint audio-video generation, enabling high-fidelity and consistent personalization. Specifically, we introduce a data curation pipeline that automatically extracts identity-bearing information with paired annotations across audio and visual modalities, covering diverse scenarios from single-subject to multi-subject interactions. We further propose a flexible and scalable identity injection mechanism for single- and multi-subject scenarios, in which both facial appearance and vocal timbre act as identity-bearing control signals. Moreover, in light of modality disparity, we design a multi-stage training strategy to accelerate convergence and enforce cross-modal coherence. Experiments demonstrate the superiority of the proposed framework. For more details and qualitative results, please refer to our webpage: [Identity-as-Presence](https://chen-yingjie.github.io/projects/Identity-as-Presence).

## 1 Introduction

![Refer to caption](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.17889v3/x1.png)

Figure 1: Identity-as-Presence supports single-subject and multi-subject personalized joint audio-video generation for facial appearance and vocal timbre.

Recent advances in audio-visual generation have established a strong foundation for high-fidelity multimodal synthesis. Building on this progress, emerging applications have created increasing demand for personalized video generation that faithfully replicates both the facial appearance and vocal timbre of real individuals. This trend highlights the need for models with cameo capabilities, enabling a specific person to speak arbitrary content and appear in diverse scenes conditioned on user input. Such functionality is critical for applications including virtual avatars and digital identity preservation, which require precise and disentangled control over facial identity and voice characteristics.

A straightforward solution is a multi-stage cascade. It first generates audio and video independently, and then performs audio-driven lip synchronization. However, such sequential pipelines impose rigid visual constraints and offer limited support for cinematic dynamics. They also model audio mainly through lip motion rather than holistic sound, which weakens overall audio-visual coherence. These limitations make end-to-end generation a more promising paradigm for unified audio-visual synthesis.

Despite this potential, building a unified cameo model remains challenging. Identity-controllable audio-video generation requires stable cross-modal identity consistency and flexible voice-face binding in multi-identity scenarios. In addition, high-quality identity-labeled audio-visual datasets are scarce, making stable and scalable training difficult. Existing identity injection methods remain largely unimodal. In audio generation, most approaches rely on autoregressive (AR) voice cloning frameworks, which excel at reproducing the voice timbre of a target individual from a reference utterance \[ren2019fastspeech, ren2020fastspeech, wang2023neural\]. While diffusion-based models have recently emerged in speech and music synthesis with limited control over individual identity \[gong2025ace, jiang2025diffrhythm\]. In video generation, facial identity is typically preserved by encoding reference images through cross-attention \[ye2023ip, he2024id, yuan2025identity\], symmetric network branches \[zhang2023adding, hu2023animateanyone\], or token-wise concatenation \[liu2025phantom, fei2025skyreels, xue2025stand\]. These methods enable faithful visual identity retention but operate solely in the visual domain. While recent works have made strides in multi-identity spatial composition via region-based attention masks \[wang2025interacthuman, deng2025magref\] or anchored prompts \[liang2025movie\], extending this capability to joint audio-video generation remains an open challenge. Most critically, the cross-modal binding of multiple individuals is largely unexplored. Unlike spatial composition in static images, joint audio-visual generation requires the model to dynamically associate specific voice timbres with their corresponding facial appearances over time. Current systems lack a unified mechanism for such cross-modal identity alignment, resulting in inconsistent or ambiguous identity mapping, especially in multi-subject scenarios. As a result, the problem of coherent, fine-grained control over multiple audio-visual identities remains fundamentally unsolved.

To this end, we present Identity-as-Presence, a unified and scalable framework for appearance- and voice-personalized joint audio-video generation. To address the critical data bottleneck, we introduce a fully automated data curation pipeline that constructs high-quality, identity-labeled audio-visual pairs without manual annotation, ensuring precise face-voice alignment for complex multi-identity interactions. To enable multimodal identity injection, we propose a unified identity injection mechanism. Specifically, visual (one-shot or multi-view facial images) and auditory identity control signals are encoded and aligned through shared identity embeddings and structured spatiotemporal positional embeddings. These embeddings ensure consistent face-voice association for multiple individuals in complex scenarios while effectively distinguishing identities and preserving structural coherence. The resulting representations are then processed by a dual-tower diffusion transformer, where a decoupled parameterization with asymmetric self-attention is introduced to stabilize optimization. Furthermore, to leverage heterogeneous data across modalities and promote progressive audio-visual alignment, we design a multi-stage training strategy. The model first learns unimodal identity injection on large-scale unimodal data, then jointly optimizes multimodal identity injection, and finally performs fine-tuning for multi-view identity modeling. Together, these designs enable high-fidelity and coherent synthesis of personalized multi-subject audio-visual content with precise identity control. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of audio quality, video fidelity, and cross-modal alignment.

Our main contributions are threefold.

- •
  We introduce a fully automated data curation pipeline that constructs high-quality, identity-labeled audio-visual pairs without manual annotation. It effectively alleviates the critical scarcity of paired multimodal data, providing a robust foundation for learning complex multi-identity interactions.
- •
  We propose a unified identity injection mechanism comprising multimodal information representation, structured spatiotemporal alignment, and decoupled attention parameterization. Together, these components resolve identity entanglement while ensuring strict cross-modal alignment.
- •
  We devise a multi-stage training strategy to mitigate optimization instability arising from cross-modal discrepancies. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance in audio generation, visual synthesis, and cross-modal alignment.

Our dataset and model will be publicly released to promote transparency, reproducibility, and further research in the AIGC community.

## 2 Related Work

Identity-Aware Video Generation. Prior work on identity-aware video generation either injects facial identity via explicit encoders or regularizes identity consistency within diffusion backbones. ID-animator \[he2024id\] conditions a text-to-video diffusion model with a lightweight face adapter and learnable facial latent queries, while ConsistID \[yuan2025identity\] enforces temporal identity coherence through frequency decomposition in diffusion transformers. Subject-consistent frameworks, such as Phantom \[liu2025phantom\], SkyReels-A2 \[fei2025skyreels\] and VACE \[jiang2025vace\], concatenate reference conditions with noisy latents and perform full-sequence denoising to stabilize identity over time. With video foundation models, multimodal customization methods such as HunyuanCustom \[hu2025hunyuancustom\] and ConceptMaster \[huang2025conceptmaster\] integrate conditions via modality-specific modules, but often incur substantial training cost. Stand-In \[xue2025stand\] introduces a lightweight, plug-and-play identity control design to reduce trainable parameters. However, these approaches focus exclusively on visual fidelity, thereby overlooking the holistic nature of identity that binds facial features with specific vocal characteristics. In contrast, our approach achieves multimodal identity-aware joint audio-video generation, ensuring cross-modal identity consistency.

Identity-Aware Audio Generation. Recent advancements in text-to-speech (TTS) have achieved human-level naturalness and high-fidelity speaker cloning \[ren2019fastspeech, ren2020fastspeech, kim2020glow, kong2020hifi, tan2024naturalspeech\], generally falling into two paradigms: Autoregressive (AR) and Non-Autoregressive (NAR). AR models, exemplified by VALL-E \[wang2023neural\], treat TTS as a language modeling task, achieving remarkable speaker similarity but often suffering from slow inference speeds due to sequential processing. In contrast, NAR models offer superior efficiency through parallel processing. Prominent flow matching models like Voicebox \[ju2024naturalspeech\] and Matcha-TTS \[mehta2024matcha\] condition flow trajectories on text and audio, while E2 TTS \[eskimez2024e2\] simplified the pipeline by discarding phoneme alignments in favor of direct subjec-level modeling. F5-TTS \[chen2025f5\] further refined this architecture by introducing Sway Sampling to rectify alignment flaws and achieve robust, high-fidelity zero-shot generation. However, these methods typically rely on audio infilling for voice cloning, a paradigm ill-suited for the arbitrary text generation required in our Cameo scenario. While joint audio-video models favor diffusion backbones, adapting them for robust voice injection remains challenging. To address this, we introduce a streamlined token-concatenation mechanism for voice identity injection. Remarkably, with minimal architectural modifications, our approach achieves voice cloning performance comparable to state-of-the-art NAR TTS models.

Joint Audio-Visual Generation. To bridge the gap between visual and auditory generation, recent research has pivoted towards joint audio-video generation. Consequently, unified frameworks have emerged as a promising direction to model the intricate dependencies between sight and sound. Pioneering works \[ltx-2, uniavgen, universe, javisdit, low2025ovi\] attempt to synthesize both modalities simultaneously within a single model. These methods predominantly adopt a dual-tower paradigm. Ovi \[low2025ovi\] and LTX-2 \[ltx-2\] employ a symmetric architecture, while JavisDit \[javisdit\], Universe-1 \[universe\], and UniAVGen \[uniavgen\] employ asymmetric designs augmented with intricate interaction modules to facilitate cross-modal fusion. However, despite these architectural innovations, existing unified methods often lack precise control over specific identities and struggle with identity entanglement in multi-subject scenarios, leading to erroneous face-voice bindings such as voice swapping. In contrast, we propose a unified framework with a multimodal identity injection approach, enabling coherent, multi-identity generation where flexible visual inputs are strictly aligned with their corresponding vocal timbres.

## 3 Identity-as-Presence

We propose Identity-as-Presence, a unified framework for joint audio-video generation that achieves coherent multi-identity control, consisting of three key aspects: an automated data curation pipeline for extracting identity-labeled audio-visual clips, a unified identity injection approach that independently modulates visual and auditory identity features, and a multi-stage training strategy designed to address modality disparity and ensure stable convergence.

### 3.1 Preliminaries

To address the distinct dynamics of visual and auditory modalities, a dual-tower architecture is employed, facilitating cross-modal interaction via attention mechanisms. Let $`v_{0}`$ and $`a_{0}`$ denote the clean latents encoded by respective VAEs. Independent probability paths are defined via linear interpolation with Gaussian noise $`v_{1},a_{1}\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)`$:

```math
v_{t}=(1-t)v_{0}+tv_{1},\quad a_{t}=(1-t)a_{0}+ta_{1},
```

where $`t\in[0,1]`$. These paths induce target velocity fields $`u_{t}^{v}=v_{1}-v_{0}`$ and $`u_{t}^{a}=a_{1}-a_{0}`$. To enable personalized generation, the model is conditioned on disentangled visual and auditory identity signals $`c^{v}`$ and $`c^{a}`$. Consequently, the joint optimization objective is a weighted sum of two flow matching losses:

```math
\mathcal{L}_{\text{joint}}=\mathbb{E}_{t,v_{0},a_{0},v_{1},a_{1}}\left[\|\hat{u}_{\theta}^{v}(v_{t},a_{t},t,c^{v})-u_{t}^{v}\|^{2}_{2}+\lambda\|\hat{u}_{\theta}^{a}(v_{t},a_{t},t,c^{a})-u_{t}^{a}\|^{2}_{2}\right],
```

where $`\hat{u}_{\theta}^{v}`$ and $`\hat{u}_{\theta}^{a}`$ denote the predicted velocity fields conditioned on their respective identity controls, and $`\lambda`$ balances the learning between the two modalities.

### 3.2 Data Curation Pipeline

![Refer to caption](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.17889v3/figures/data_curation.png)

Figure 2: Overview of data curation pipeline for constructing identity-labeled audio-visual data from raw videos. The process involves isolating both visual and auditory identity-specific signals from raw videos, synthesizing comprehensive captions via MLLMs, and rigorously matching audio-visual identities to guarantee precise alignment across video clips to ensure high-fidelity identity consistency.

High-quality, identity-consistent data is the cornerstone of personalized audio-visual generation. To address the scarcity of paired multimodal data, we develop a fully automated data curation pipeline. As illustrated in Figure 2, the pipeline operates in three cascaded phases: (1) Multimodal Identity Extraction, which extracts fine-grained identity-bearing information from raw videos; (2) Multimodal Captioning, which leverages multimodal large language models (MLLMs) to synthesize comprehensive textual descriptions; and (3) Audio-Visual Identity Matching, which associates identities across clips for accurate identity injection.

1.  1.  Multimodal Identity Extraction. For the visual stream, we first apply multi-object tracking to isolate individual subjects within raw videos. Frame-wise human detection is performed using YOLOv11 \[khanam2024yolov11\], followed by long-term tracking with MOTRv2 \[zhang2023motrv2\]. For each tracked subject, we perform face detection and estimate FLAME \[flame\] parameters using SMIRK \[retsinas2024smirk\], capturing dense 3D facial geometry and expression details. The resulting 3D parameters are then used to extract high-quality one-shot or diverse multi-view reference images, which serve as robust facial identity control signals for our generative model.

    Handling the audio modality requires separating mixed vocal tracks. We use Demucs \[defossez2019demucs\] to separate vocal and background components and apply 3D-Speaker \[chen20253d\] for speaker diarization, isolating the distinct voice of each speaker. Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) is used to transcribe the spoken content. To align facial appearance with vocal timbre, we employ SyncNet \[chung2016out\] to compute the synchronization confidence between the separated audio and visual lip movements. Only pairs with high synchronization scores are retained, ensuring that the extracted voice timbre is correctly associated with the corresponding facial identity.

2.  2.  Multimodal Captioning. Finally, we use MLLMs to generate textual descriptions for each video clip. Specifically, we feed each triplet consisting of the video clip, the extracted facial images for each subject, and the ASR transcripts into Qwen3-Omni \[qwen-omni\]. Using a carefully designed prompt template, we instruct the model to generate comprehensive descriptions covering camera movements, scene context, ambience and sound effects, and subject descriptions, including subject appearance, actions, expressions, and speech content. Moreover, to establish robust subject anchors, we explicitly require the model to bind physical appearance attributes and spoken content to unique identifiers for each subject. As shown in the right part of Figure 2, these structured captions serve as the text prompts for our joint audio-video generation model.
3.  3.  Audio-Visual Identity Matching. To ensure the model learns to inject identity rather than simply copy and paste, we construct training pairs via an extra matching phase. Specifically, the reference facial images are supposed to be diverse in identity-irrelevant attributes such as head pose and expression, and the reference audio must not share causal transcription links with the target video. To achieve this, we implement a strict cross-clip matching strategy. First, we group video clips by visual identity using face embeddings extracted by ArcFace \[deng2019arcface\]. However, visual consistency does not guarantee vocal consistency due to dubbing or voice-overs. Therefore, we further refine these groups using speaker embeddings extracted by ERes2Net \[chen2024eres2netv2\]. We compute the cosine similarity between speaker embeddings to filter out pairs with low similarity, ensuring that our training pairs share the same appearance and voice identity while containing diverse, non-overlapping speech content.

Our data curation pipeline ensures precise alignment between facial appearance and vocal timbre, through which we construct a large-scale identity-labeled audio-visual dataset encompassing diverse scenarios ranging from single-subject to multi-subject interactions. The resulting dataset is denoted as $`\mathcal{D}=\{(v,a,c^{v},`$ $`c^{a},\text{txt})\}`$, where $`v`$ and $`a`$ represent the video and audio modalities, and $`c^{v}`$ and $`c^{a}`$ correspond to facial appearance and vocal timbre. $`\mathcal{D}`$ serves as the foundation for our model, providing robust supervision to learn the disentangled yet synchronized identity signals required by our Identity-as-Presence framework.

### 3.3 Network Architecture

![Refer to caption](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.17889v3/x2.png)

Figure 3: The overall dual-tower DiT network architecture and training framework. The model has five inputs: video, audio, video identity, audio identity, and structured caption. We first extract latents for each modality, apply identity embedding to identity latents, then organize latents with structured position embedding. In DiT, we use asymmetric self-attention for decoupled parameterization. The training contains three strages. Stage 1 for unimodal identity, stage 2 for joint multimodel identity training, and stage 3 for multi-view identity fine-tuning.

To achieve high-fidelity joint audio-video generation, we build our framework upon a dual-tower Diffusion Transformer (DiT) architecture. A critical challenge in this paradigm is the effective injection of identity control signals, specifically facial appearance and vocal timbre, into the generative process in a manner that is both disentangled and coherent. To address this, we propose a unified injection strategy that integrates multimodel information representation, spatiotemporal alignment, and attention mechanisms, as shown in Figure 3.

#### 3.3.1 Multimodel Identity Injection.

Instead of employing auxiliary control networks or adapters, we treat identity signals as additional input tokens and seamlessly integrate them into the input sequence of the DiT backbone. This unified approach allows for the joint optimization of multimodal content generation and identity preservation.

Let $`m\in\{v,a\}`$ denote the modality index. Let $`z_{t}^{m}\in\mathbb{R}^{L_{t}^{m}\times D}`$ be the sequence of noisy latent tokens at diffusion timestep $`t`$, and $`c^{m}`$ be the corresponding raw reference signal. Specifically, for the visual branch ($`m=v`$), $`c^{v}`$ comprises reference facial images (supporting both one-shot and multi-view inputs), and for the auditory branch ($`m=a`$), $`c^{a}`$ consists of a reference audio clip. We first encode these control signals into discrete tokens $`r^{m}`$ using modality-specific VAE encoders:

```math
r^{m}=\text{Patchify}(\mathcal{E}_{m}(c^{m}))\in\mathbb{R}^{L_{r}^{m}\times D}.
```

Shared Identity Embeddings. To enable robust multi-identity personalized generation, we must ensure that each voice timbre is correctly associated with its corresponding facial appearance. To this end, we introduce learnable Identity Embeddings to explicitly bind these modalities at the token level. For a scene with $`K`$ distinct identities, we assign a unique vector $`e_{id}^{(k)}`$ to the $`k`$-th identity. This embedding is injected into the reference tokens via element-wise addition:

```math
\hat{r}_{m}^{(k)}=r_{m}^{(k)}+e_{id}^{(k)},\quad m\in\{v,a\}.
```

Since $`e_{id}^{(k)}`$ is shared between $`r_{v}^{(k)}`$ and $`r_{a}^{(k)}`$, it serves as an explicit binding signal that guides the model to associate the visual tokens of identity $`k`$ with the auditory tokens of the same identity.

The identity-bound reference tokens $`\hat{r}^{m}`$ are then concatenated with the noisy latents $`z_{t}^{m}`$ along the sequence dimension to form $`X^{m}\in\mathbb{R}^{(L_{r}^{m}+L_{t}^{m})\times D}`$ as the composite input for each tower:

```math
X^{m}=[\hat{r}^{m};z_{t}^{m}].
```

This token-wise concatenation imposes a symmetric inductive bias, allowing the model to flexibly attend to fine-grained features, aggregating multi-view facial details or extracting spectral formants, with minimal architectural overhead.

Structured Position Embeddings. Proper positional injection is crucial for the model to distinguish between identities and maintain structural coherence. For the video and audio latent tokens $`z_{t}^{v}`$ and $`z_{t}^{a}`$, we apply standard factorized 3D RoPE \[su2021roformer\] and scale the audio temporal channels to align with the video frame rate. For the identity tokens $`\hat{r}^{v}`$ and $`\hat{r}^{a}`$, positional assignment requires a specialized strategy to ensure numerical stability and cross-modal binding. To this end, we propose a virtual temporal extension mechanism that assigns deterministic coordinates to identity tokens beyond the generation timeline.

Recognizing that facial appearance identity $`\hat{r}^{v}`$ is inherently encoded in the spatial domain while vocal timbre identity $`\hat{r}^{a}`$ unfolds along the temporal dimension, we establish a strict geometric alignment to bridge these heterogeneous modalities. We map the spatially organized visual tokens $`\hat{r}^{v}`$ to virtual temporal indices, assigning $`T_{v,\text{max}}`$ to visual tokens of the first identity, $`T_{v,\text{max}}+1`$ to the second, and continuing sequentially. Correspondingly, the auditory identity tokens $`\hat{r}^{a}`$ are arranged along the temporal axis starting from $`T_{a,\text{max}}`$. We synchronize this placement such that the $`k`$-th auditory identity (at $`T_{a,\text{max}}+(k-1)\times L`$, where $`L`$ denotes the maximum length of the audio reference) aligns strictly with the $`k`$-th visual identity. This creates an invariant geometric anchor, allowing the attention mechanism to utilize relative positional distances as a strong inductive bias for binding spatial facial appearance with the corresponding temporal vocal timbre.

We further propose a strategy for multi-view facial tokens. Instead of linearly concatenating reference images, which often leads to out-of-distribution spatial coordinates and frequency extrapolation artifacts, we employ a dense near-square packing strategy for multi-view facial images. We organize the multi-view images into a compact grid layout within $`(H,W)`$ before patchification. The resulting visual tokens $`\hat{r}^{v}`$ are then assigned a specific virtual temporal position. This layout constrains the maximum spatial frequencies to a stable range seen during training, significantly improving the robustness of the rotary embedding against spatial distortions.

Asymmetric Self-Attention with Decoupled Parameterization. Standard self-attention layers typically share projection weights across all tokens. However, given the distinct semantic roles of the reference signals (condition) and the noisy latents (generation target), sharing weights may lead to optimization conflicts. To address this, we propose a decoupled parameterization strategy combined with an asymmetric attention mask.

Let $`X\in\mathbb{R}^{L\times D}`$ be the input sequence. We divide the token indices into a reference set $`\Omega_{r}`$ and a noisy latent set $`\Omega_{z}`$, where $`\Omega_{r}\cup\Omega_{z}=\{1,\dots,L\}`$. To learn specialized representations, we use separate projection weights for the two groups: $`\mathcal{W}^{(r)}`$ for reference tokens and $`\mathcal{W}^{(z)}`$ for noisy tokens. Each token is projected using the weights corresponding to its set, producing the matrices $`Q,K,V\in\mathbb{R}^{L\times d}`$. This decoupling allows the model to learn identity-preserving and denoising features independently.

To control information flow, we introduce a structural mask $`M`$ in the attention computation:

```math
\text{Attn}(Q,K,V)=\text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^{\top}}{\sqrt{d}}+M\right)V,\ \ M_{i,j}=\begin{cases}-\infty&i\in\Omega_{r},j\in\Omega_{z},\\
0&\text{otherwise.}\end{cases}
```

With this mask, noisy latents can extract identity cues from references, whereas reference features remain unaffected by diffusion noise, preserving the integrity of the control signals during generation.

### 3.4 Training Strategy

To leverage heterogeneous data across modalities and facilitate progressive audio-visual alignment, we adopt a multi-stage training strategy motivated by disparities in data availability. Identity-labeled audio data (e.g., TTS corpora) is the most abundant, identity-labeled video data is more limited due to filtering requirements, and paired identity-labeled audio-visual data is the scarcest because of strict synchronization and semantic constraints. Directly training on limited paired data risks overfitting and poor identity diversity. We therefore propose a progressive learning framework with three stages: unimodal identity training, joint multimodal identity training, and multi-view identity fine-tuning.

Stage 1: Unimodal Identity Training. We first decouple training to fully exploit large-scale unimodal data and learn identity-specific priors before cross-modal alignment. The audio tower is trained on large TTS datasets to model diverse vocal timbres and prosody, enabling robust voice identity injection. Notably, we find that the unimodal pre-training stage is the optimal window for instilling robust multilingual capabilities. This stage imposes lower data requirements while ensuring that the acquired capabilities are well-preserved during post-training. We conduct experiments on TTS benchmarks to demonstrate that after the unimodal training stage, our audio tower achieves comparable performance to non-autoregressive (NAR) TTS models, as shown in Table 1. In parallel, the video tower is trained on identity-labeled video datasets to learn appearance and motion dynamics. During this stage, cross-modal fusion layers are masked, allowing each modality to converge optimally on its respective data.

Stage 2: Joint Multimodal Identity Training. Then, we perform joint multimodal training using curated paired identity-labeled audio-visual data. The model is initialized from Stage 1 and cross-modal fusion layers are activated to learn temporal alignment between speech and facial motion. This decoupling allows large unimodal datasets to provide identity diversity while a smaller paired set enables precise audio-visual synchronization.

Stage 3: Multi-view Identity Fine-tuning. To overcome the limitations of 2D one-shot generation, we introduce a lightweight fine-tuning stage using a small set of high-quality multi-view identity data. This stage improves spatial awareness and injects holistic identity consistency, enabling avatars to maintain structural integrity and photorealism under large pose variations.

### 4.1 Experimental Settings

Evaluation Benchmark. To rigorously assess joint audio-video generation, we introduce a benchmark of 100 curated triplets, split into 70 single-subject and 30 multi-subject scenarios. Each entry contains a triplet of reference facial images (one-shot/multi-view), an audio clip, and the corresponding text prompt, spanning diverse subjects and scenarios. Unlike conventional single-modality benchmarks, this dataset serves as a holistic testbed for cross-modal identity preservation, challenging models to maintain high fidelity and synchronization in complex multi-subject settings beyond limited training distributions.

Evaluation Metrics. Audio Quality: We measure speech intelligibility using Word Error Rate (WER) via SenseVoice \[sensevoice\], semantic consistency with CLAP \[clap\], and perceptual similarity to the ground truth with Fréchet Distance (FD), respectively. Audio production quality (PQ) is evaluated using Audiobox \[audiobox\], while speaker identity similarity (AID-SIM) is calculated using the cosine similarity of the WavLM embeddings \[chen2022wavlm\]. Visual Fidelity: We use Aesthetic Quality (AES), Dynamic Degree (DD), and Overall Consistency (OC) from VBench \[vbench\] to measure the artistic and beauty value, the degree of dynamics, and overall video-text consistency. Facial identity preservation (VID-SIM) is evaluated using ArcFace \[deng2019arcface\] feature similarity. Audio-Visual Consistency: We employ SyncNet \[chung2016out\] to measure lip-sync accuracy via confidence (Sync-C) and distance (Sync-D) scores. Additionally, ImageBind \[imagebind\] is used to assess the alignment between visual and auditory events in complex scenarios.

Implementation Details. We employ a dual-tower architecture following Ovi \[low2025ovi\] and initialize with its pre-trained weights. AdamW \[loshchilov2017decoupled\] with $`\beta_{1}=0.9`$, $`\beta_{2}=0.999`$ and $`\epsilon=10^{-8}`$ are used as optimizer, with learning rate of 1e-4 and batch size of 64. For unimodal training, we train 12K iterations on 150K hours of TTS data on audio tower, and 8K iterations on 100K video clips on video tower. For multimodal training, we train 5K steps on 50K identity-labeled audio-visual data on the final dual-tower model. Finally, we fine-tune the model using 10K multi-view video clips to enable multi-view injection.

### 4.2 Comparison with State-of-the-Art Methods

We compare the proposed method with state-of-the-art methods that include identity-sensitive video generation models, Phantom \[liu2025phantom\], HunyuanCustom \[hu2025hunyuancustom\] and Stand-In \[xue2025stand\] and joint audio-video generation models, Ovi \[low2025ovi\], LTX-2 \[ltx-2\] and UniAVGen \[uniavgen\]. Note that UniAVGen only supports single-subject voice ID injection. For a fair comparison under identity-aware T2AV settings, we employ Qwen-Image \[wu2025qwen\] to generate the first frame for baselines lacking native ID support, unifying the task as I2AV generation. All samples are generated at 720p resolution for 5 seconds. As reported in Table 2, our method outperforms others on audio quality, video fidelity, and audio-visual consistency. Quantitative results are presented in Table 3. From Table 3, we observe that both subject anchors and shared identity embeddings facilitate achieving more stable multi-subject personalization generation.

As shown in Figure 5, removing subject anchors (w/o SA) leads to identity confusion. Without these anchors, the model may erroneously entangle speech content with speaker identity (top-left) or generate subjects that share partial appearance features (top-right). In the absence of identity embeddings (w/o IE), the model fails to align specific Face IDs with their corresponding Voice IDs. This leads to either unintended overlap (bottom-left), where subjects speak simultaneously due to a lack of turn-taking, or voice-face mismatch (bottom-right), where a face is driven by the wrong voice.

Regarding the training strategy, we report results under the same training iterations for both one-stage and two-stage settings, noting that multi-view finetuning requires slightly more continuous iterations. Results in Table 3 show that our training strategy effectively leverages unimodal data to accelerate convergence, and multi-view extension can be enabled with minimal fine-tuning on a small set of multi-view data.

## 5 Conclusion

In this work, we presented Identity-as-Presence, a pioneering framework that addresses the critical challenges of personalized joint audio-video generation. By moving beyond the limitations of rigid cascaded pipelines and unimodal approaches, we established a robust end-to-end framework capable of simultaneous, high-fidelity injection of both facial appearance and vocal timbre. Our approach effectively resolves the complexities of cross-modal consistency and multi-identity attribute binding through three synergistic innovations, _i.e_., an automated data curation pipeline that alleviates the scarcity of identity-labeled audio-visual paired data, a unified identity injection mechanism that ensures precise face-voice association even in complex multi-subject scenarios, and a multi-stage training strategy that harmonizes the optimization of heterogeneous modalities. Extensive evaluations demonstrate that our framework significantly outperforms existing methods in generating coherent, identity-aware audio-visual content. We believe this work sets a new standard for personalized multimodal synthesis, paving the way for advanced applications.
